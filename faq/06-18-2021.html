- What do you mean Type Driven Development? Don’t you mean “Test...”?!

No! I meant “Type”’. Types or strongly typed programming languages provide error checking phases to eliminate unintentional errors in your code. So why not use it? Compile time will increase you say? Well, nothing is free! The cost of proving correctness and code consistency and avoiding future mistakes is a little bit of not so wasted time.

- What’s the point of TDD or Tests then?

Tests are useful, too! I see Tests(unit, integration) as a way to make sure of Softwares’ consistency. Every User Story/feature requirement needs to be properly understood; Therefore that raises a need for specification assurance. Tests are the best tool for this. You write down what your PO wants and then it will assure you of its requirements and existence. Types help you avoid a lot of corner case scenarios, like input validation for every possible input.

- Agile? What do you think?

Agile Manifesto is all about avoiding bureaucracy, having direct communication and a proper feedback loop to learn from your mistakes. Between Kanban and Scrum I’d pick Kanban because of the controversialness around Scrum and how it has turned into a cult.

- Object Oriented or Functional paradigm? Why?

FP. The traits that generally are mentioned alongside FP; Immutability, Referential transparency, Separation of Side effects, and Type Systems. All of these help us to avoid bugs. Especially when you are doing concurrency.

- So Why do you hate OOP?!

Who said I hate OOP? It’s just another tool. Personally I think mathematical projection of our ideas map better into FP paradigms. That doesn’t mean OOP is bad. We have to evaluate it to our needs. Actually, I am a huge fan of SOLID principles. I’ll do my best to obey them as much as I can, even though it is an OOP term it is still useful in the FP world.

- What do you think of the Story ownership model?

I’d encourage it! When you are responsible for a feature you will be sure the output is as expected but when you are doing only part of the task you think others will take care of the issues coming out of it.

- Pair programming or Code Review?

I’d go with Pair Programming most of the time but sometimes you need some alone time which is understandable.

Pairing with someone in development reduces the possibility of making mistakes as it is an active engagement task which is fun as you are coming up with ideas with the partner. It’s a team effort to deliver the result. On the other hand, Code Review is a passive activity which is not fun. Imagine someone else always finding your mistakes! Especially if they don’t know how to communicate it, it will increase the tension in the team.
